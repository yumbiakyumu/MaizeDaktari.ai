    Common Rust Ravages Maize Production in Kenya

    Common rust, a fungal disease, has been a major scourge on maize production in Kenya. This condition has been witnessed in many maize-growing regions, particularly in the eastern and western counties of the country. The disease causes the leaves and stems to wither and dry up, ultimately leading to significant yield losses. The government and agricultural experts are making efforts to combat this menace, including exploring alternative methods to control common rust. Failure to address this issue could severely impact the nation's food security, as maize is a staple crop for millions of Kenyans.
